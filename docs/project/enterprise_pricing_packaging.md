# GovAI Enterprise Pricing and Packaging

## Purpose

This document defines an initial commercial packaging and pricing strategy for GovAI.

GovAI combines an open source governance core with enterprise capabilities, hosted services, and advisory offerings. The goal of this document is to provide a structured path from public adoption to commercial revenue.

## Product Packaging Strategy

GovAI should follow an open core model.

### Open Source Core

The public repository should include:

- Decision-level governance engine
- Python CLI
- Rust audit server
- Local Docker Compose demo
- Documentation
- Example repositories
- Community support

### Hosted Cloud Service

A managed GovAI service may provide:

- Hosted compliance gate
- Managed audit infrastructure
- Usage metering and billing
- Team management
- Operational monitoring
- Simplified onboarding

### Enterprise Edition

Enterprise offerings may include:

- Single sign-on
- Advanced RBAC
- Private deployment support
- Custom retention and storage options
- Dedicated support
- Security reviews
- Custom integrations

### Advisory and Training

Additional offerings may include:

- Architecture workshops
- Governance design reviews
- AI Act mapping sessions
- Training and certification

## Suggested Pricing Structure

### Starter

Designed for small teams and early adopters.

Indicative pricing:

- Free open source usage
- Optional low-cost hosted plan

### Professional

Designed for growing teams.

Indicative pricing:

- Team subscriptions
- Metered hosted usage
- Priority support

### Enterprise

Designed for regulated and large organizations.

Indicative pricing:

- Custom contracts
- Private deployment
- Security review support
- Dedicated onboarding

## Revenue Streams

Potential revenue sources include:

1. Hosted subscriptions
2. Metered compliance usage
3. Enterprise licenses
4. Professional services
5. Training
6. Certification
7. Research partnerships

## Target Customers

Primary customer segments:

- AI engineering teams
- MLOps teams
- Regulated enterprises
- Financial institutions
- Healthcare organizations
- Government and public sector
- Technology companies building high-impact AI systems

## Sales Narrative

GovAI should be positioned as infrastructure that turns governance requirements into enforceable technical controls.

Key messages:

- Decision-level auditability
- Fail-closed governance
- Evidence-gated deployment
- Governance-as-code
- AI Act readiness support
- Open source transparency

## Adoption Funnel

1. Discover the open source project
2. Run the local demo
3. Evaluate example repositories
4. Conduct a pilot
5. Adopt hosted or enterprise offerings
6. Expand usage across teams

## Pilot to Production Path

A typical commercial journey may be:

1. Technical evaluation
2. Proof of concept
3. Pilot deployment
4. Enterprise rollout
5. Ongoing subscriptions and support

## Pricing Considerations

Pricing should reflect:

- Risk reduction
- Auditability improvements
- Compliance acceleration
- Operational convenience
- Expert support

## Success Metrics

Commercial success indicators include:

- Pilot conversions
- Monthly recurring revenue
- Expansion revenue
- Retention
- Community growth
- Enterprise references

## Summary

GovAI is well suited to an open core commercial model. Open source adoption builds trust, while hosted services, enterprise features, and expert services create multiple monetization paths.
